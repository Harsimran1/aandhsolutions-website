## Canadian compliance notes buyers should verify

For AI medical scribe procurement in Canada, do not stop at feature demos. Confirm legal and operational controls in writing.

1. **PIPEDA:** Private-sector handling of personal information in commercial activity must meet PIPEDA requirements.
2. **Provincial health privacy laws:** Depending on jurisdiction and care setting, provincial health privacy statutes can apply (for example Ontario PHIPA).
3. **HIPAA:** If your workflow touches U.S. covered entities, U.S. patients, or cross-border service models, HIPAA obligations may be required contractually and operationally.
4. **Data residency and transfers:** Confirm where data is stored/processed and whether cross-border transfer is enabled by default.
5. **BAA/DPA terms:** Ensure contractual terms clearly define permitted use, retention, deletion, and incident response.
6. **Human review controls:** AI output should remain draft support; clinicians must review and sign before chart finalization.

## How to choose the right AI scribe for your clinic

Before purchasing, ask:

1. Which Canadian privacy laws does your deployment model map to, and how is this documented?
2. Do you provide configurable retention, deletion, and no-training-on-PHI controls?
3. Which EMRs are truly integrated vs. copy/paste workflows?
4. What does onboarding look like by specialty and documentation style?
5. What measurable charting-time reduction have similar clinics achieved post-rollout?
