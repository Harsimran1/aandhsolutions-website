If you are evaluating AI automation partners in Ottawa, this list is written for buyers who need production-grade implementation — not a discovery workshop followed by a slide deck.

Ottawa's AI market has distinct characteristics driven by the federal government's presence: a high concentration of public sector clients, strong emphasis on security and data privacy compliance (particularly around PIPEDA and government cloud requirements), and a technology community shaped by firms like Shopify, Kinaxis, and a growing cluster of defence and govtech companies. For AI automation buyers, this means the Ottawa market has real depth in secure, compliant implementation — but it also means strategy-heavy consulting is more common than in pure commercial tech markets. The agencies on this list are evaluated on delivery, not just advisory capability.

## How we built this shortlist

This is an editorial ranking based on:

1. Ottawa presence (headquartered in Ottawa, local office, or confirmed Ottawa delivery focus).
2. Demonstrated AI automation and workflow capability — not only strategy or consulting.
3. Ability to ship systems that connect real tools: CRMs, ERPs, communication platforms, and custom APIs.
4. Public proof points including case studies, Clutch reviews, platform profiles, or client references.

## How to evaluate an AI automation partner

Before committing, these questions separate real implementers from strategy-only firms:

1. Can you show a live automation system you shipped that is still running in production?
2. What happens when an automation step fails — how does the system recover, and how do you find out?
3. Which specific tools in our stack have you integrated before?
4. How do you scope and price a project — fixed scope, time-and-materials, or retainer?
5. What does handoff look like — do we get documentation, or are we dependent on you to make changes?
